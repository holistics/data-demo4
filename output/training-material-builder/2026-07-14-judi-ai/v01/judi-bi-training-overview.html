The goal

Adoption, not attendance

Success is the Judi Dataset becoming the first place each person looks when a client asks how their applications are doing. Three things move that needle, in order, and every session lands the third before the person leaves.

01

Confidence in the numbers

People trust Funded Rate, Auto-Approval Rate, and the funded amount because they can read the definition straight from the info icon and the shared glossary.

02

A question they already ask

Start from the real question the role cares about — "how many applications funded last month?" — then show the view that answers it.

03

One self-serve win

The person filters, drills, or asks AI themselves and it works. That first win is what converts a hesitant user.

Two gotchas everyone must know

⚠ The two things that surprise people

  • One application has many decisions. Slicing a plain count by an adjudication field over-counts superseded decisions. Use Applications (Current Decision) instead.
  • Credit scores are partial. Score averages exclude no-hits and some are sparse (~1.2k apps). Read the matching Hit Rate for coverage. The trial is one tenant (vancity), and test products should be excluded.
